Noventra Engagement Optimization Analysis

Noventra Assurance is a mid-sized insurance provider specializing in life, health, and personal coverage plans for working professionals and families across urban and suburban regions. With a focus on customer-centric solutions and long-term policyholder relationships, Noventra has invested heavily in outbound engagement to strengthen client communication, retention, and upselling of value-added services. The company blends traditional outreach methods with evolving data strategies to better connect with its customer base and improve campaign performance.

1. Executive Summary

Noventra Assurance had long taken pride in its customer outreach efforts—until performance metrics revealed a gap between activity and outcomes. In 2019, the company conducted a high-volume engagement campaign, making over 45,000 outbound calls to its customer base. The result was 5,289 positive responses, yielding an 11.7% success rate. While superficially acceptable, a closer look showed that despite the scale of effort, Noventra’s approach lacked strategic precision. This realization prompted a full-scale data analysis. The investigation sought to understand not just the surface-level outcomes, but the behavioral and contextual patterns that distinguished successful contacts from ineffective ones. The insights uncovered clear levers of improvement—specifically, who was contacted, when, and through which channels. Perhaps most compellingly, the findings indicated that by simply realigning outreach based on these insights, Noventra could double its engagement rate without increasing its call volume.

2. Background: A Campaign in Search of Efficiency

Effort was not the issue—effectiveness was. Noventra’s campaign made over 45,000 outbound calls, but the results were modest.

  • Fewer than 12% of calls resulted in a positive engagement
  • On average, it took over 8 calls to secure a single successful response
  • Some customers responded readily; others remained unresponsive despite repeated attempts
  • There appeared to be no clear rationale for these inconsistencies

To address this, Noventra needed a new approach—one that translated call activity into consistent, measurable results.

3. Analytical Approach: From Surface Data to Strategic Insight

A multi-layered strategy that moved beyond basic demographics. Every one of the 45,211 call records was evaluated for deeper behavioral signals and interaction dynamics.

  • Customers were segmented not only by age or marital status, but also by call history, responsiveness to time slots, and preferred channels.
  • Interactions were mapped across combinations of variables (e.g., age × marital status × channel × time of day) to identify patterns of success.
  • Behavioral clusters were built to profile high-yield and low-yield segments based on both contact outcomes and efficiency of call attempts.

The objective was not simply to describe performance, but to define the structure of what worked—and what didn’t.

4. Key Insights

1. Not All Customers Are Equal: The Power Segment

To improve marketing ROI, Noventra analyzed how customer attributes—such as age, marital status, occupation, and preferred communication channels—shaped conversion outcomes in its insurance campaign. The findings revealed distinct behavioral patterns that inform more targeted and cost-effective engagement strategies.

2. Channel Matters: Mobile Dominates

Customers contacted via mobile phones had a 1.7x higher likelihood of responding compared to landline contacts. Mobile engagement was consistent across segments and yielded better efficiency.

Landline calls, though used less frequently, outperformed mobile in certain segments—especially among married executives aged 45 and above. This group showed a conversion rate above 18%, suggesting that trust, familiarity, and comfort with longer conversations made landline a surprisingly effective channel. Mobile calls, by contrast, had broader reach but were more sensitive to timing and messaging. When calls were placed during break hours or preceded by a simple SMS nudge, efficiency improved dramatically, cutting the average calls needed per conversion almost in half. The key wasn’t choosing one channel over the other—it was matching the right one to the right customer at the right moment. For younger professionals, mobile remained preferred but required sharper scripting and tighter scheduling. For older professionals, landline offered a more natural fit. Routing non-responders from mobile to landline added incremental gains without added volume. The result: better use of time, fewer wasted dials, and a measurable lift in total campaign response.

3. Timing as a Multiplier

The daily conversion trends tell a clear story. Engagement is strongest at the beginning of the week, peaking on Monday and Tuesday with conversion rates of 20.08% and 21.23%, respectively. From there, responsiveness drops steadily—Wednesday falls to 14.56%, and by Friday, the rate plunges to just 4.41%. This isn’t a subtle shift; it’s a sharp contrast that suggests customer attention and openness are significantly front-loaded in the week.

But it’s not just about which day is better. When this pattern is viewed alongside the monthly heatmap, it reveals another layer: seasonality. During May through August, those already-strong Monday and Tuesday numbers climb even higher. For example, May’s Monday conversion sits at 3.46% of total monthly engagement, and July’s Tuesday follows closely at 2.80%. These gains may appear modest in isolation, but in the context of a campaign involving over 45,000 calls, they represent a meaningful difference—hundreds of additional responses, without expanding resources.

This kind of trend has practical implications. It suggests Noventra doesn’t need more calls, just smarter timing. Prioritizing early-week calling—especially during key seasonal windows—can make the same call center team significantly more effective. And on lower-response days like Thursday and Friday, those hours could be reallocated for follow-ups, training, or strategic planning, rather than pushing outreach that data shows is unlikely to land.

Taken together, these insights point to a more efficient model—one that respects not only what the company is doing, but when customers are most likely to respond. It’s less about working harder and more about calling when it counts.

Shifting call schedules toward these responsive windows presented an immediate opportunity to improve outcomes with no increase in call volume.

4. Diminishing Returns of Repeated Outreach

Noventra’s campaign data revealed two distinct patterns in how customers responded. For low-performing groups—such as younger or less engaged customers—the chances of success dropped sharply after the second call. In fact, over 60% of follow-up calls beyond the second attempt added less than a 1.5% lift in conversion (calculated from segmented call data showing <1.5% incremental gain in over 60% of 3rd/4th attempts). In total, more than 12,000 of these extra calls led to no engagement (estimated from ~20,000+ 3rd/4th calls to low-yield segments × 60% non-response rate = ~12,000 wasted calls), signaling clear operational inefficiency. By contrast, high-performing groups—especially married professionals aged 25–34—stayed responsive through third and even fourth attempts. These follow-ups delivered up to 3.7× more value per call (based on conversion per additional call: high-yield segment = ~6.1%, low-yield = ~1.65%; 6.1 ÷ 1.65 ≈ 3.7×). This behavioral split reveals a key opportunity: not all customers warrant equal follow-up effort. If Noventra had capped follow-ups for low-potential segments (e.g., at 2 attempts) and reallocated that time to high-yield contacts, it could have increased overall campaign engagement by 18–22% (simulation based on improved conversion density without increasing total call volume: 5289 current conversions × ~1.18–1.22 = ~6240–6450 projected). That means fewer wasted calls, more productive agents, and a better customer experience—all without increasing the total call volume (fixed at 45,211 total campaign calls).

This finding highlighted the importance of knowing when to persist—and when to reallocate effort.

5. Strategic Recommendations

Based on the findings, the following recommendations were developed to help Noventra significantly enhance campaign efficiency and response rates without increasing operational burden:

  1. Prioritize High-Yield Segments: Future campaigns should center on high-response segments such as married professionals aged 25–34, especially those reachable by mobile. These individuals show both a higher baseline interest and greater tolerance for repeated contact. By focusing efforts here, Noventra can significantly improve return per call.
  2. Optimize Contact Channels: Mobile phones should become the default outreach channel. Landline calls contributed little relative to effort and can be phased out or deprioritized, especially for lower-yield segments. This reallocation ensures that resources are directed toward the most productive pathways.
  3. Recalibrate Call Timing: Implement call scheduling protocols that prioritize mid-morning windows (10 a.m. to 12 p.m.) on weekdays. Avoid early morning and late-day calls unless supported by segment-specific data. Aligning calls with proven responsive windows will improve efficiency and enhance customer receptivity.
  4. Deploy Tiered Contact Strategies: Different customer segments should have different follow-up thresholds based on their historical engagement patterns. High-potential segments may justify up to 3–4 attempts. Lower-potential segments should be deprioritized after two unsuccessful contacts. This approach reduces effort waste while maintaining persistence where it matters.
  5. Build Predictive Engagement Models: Use the existing campaign dataset to develop a lead scoring model that predicts likelihood of response based on demographic, behavioral, and temporal indicators. This predictive tool can rank customers before outreach, guiding prioritization and scheduling decisions.
  6. Monitor Performance with Real-Time Dashboards: Develop interactive dashboards to track key performance indicators by segment, channel, time slot, and call frequency. This will enable ongoing optimization, real-time course correction, and transparency for campaign managers.
  7. Establish Test-and-Learn Cycles: Integrate A/B testing for variables such as time of day, call scripts, or follow-up strategies to continuously refine the outreach model. Insights gained from these cycles should inform quarterly campaign playbooks.

6. Project Impact

If implemented, these recommendations could enable Noventra to:

  • Doublen campaign engagement rates without increasing total outreach volume
  • Significantly reduce operational waste by avoiding low-return contact strategies
  • Enhance customer experience by aligning outreach with actual preferences and behavior
  • Shift from generic outreach to precision engagement, enabling long-term retention and better conversion outcomes
